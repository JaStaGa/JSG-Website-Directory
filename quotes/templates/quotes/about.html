About Lil Wayne

Lil Wayne, born Dwayne Michael Carter Jr. on September 27, 1982, in New Orleans, Louisiana, is an influential American rapper, singer, and songwriter. Rising to fame as a teen with the group Hot Boys, he became a solo superstar with the success of albums like *Tha Carter III* (2008), which earned him a Grammy Award for Best Rap Album. Known for his clever wordplay, prolific mixtape releases, and impact on modern hip-hop, Lil Wayne is often credited with shaping the careers of artists like Drake and Nicki Minaj through his label, Young Money Entertainment.
